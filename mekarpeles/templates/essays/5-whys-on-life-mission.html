Justification

In the spirit of Sakichi Toyoda's 5 Why's, my purpose for wanting to create a living Universal Knowledge Map comes from my belief that, without one, the world will progress more slowly, less efficiently, and with more mistakes.

Why? For four reasons:

  1. People often talk past each other or lie to each other. Partly because they can (and it serves them to) and party because there's no central, mutually relatable point of truth by which people can align / ground / relate their understanding. Thus, a Universal Knowledge Map would mean more productive conversation: it would help us identify exact points of contention or misunderstandings, provide us with a more comprehensive means of checking the factual accuracy of statements, and allow us to derive simpler or more accurate models to describe complex or inaccessible concepts.
  2. Ubiquitous access to a Universal Knowledge Graph would reduce redundant efforts and make it more clear where gaps in knowledge are.
